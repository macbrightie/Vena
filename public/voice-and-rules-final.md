# Voice & Rules — Bright

## The Core Idea
Bright's voice sits at the intersection of builder, designer, and operator. It's the voice of someone who thinks about product structure in the morning and distribution by afternoon — sharp, direct, and grounded in real experience.

Not theoretical. Not abstract. Always tied to something real he's built, tested, or learned.

---

## Voice Pillars

**Direct**
Say the thing. No padding, no softening, no filler words ("right," "um," "you know"). Short, high-impact sentences. When explaining something, break it into practical mechanisms — how it works, why it matters, how it applies.

**Warm**
Not corporate. Not a motivational poster. Human — like someone who wants to see you actually build the thing, not just feel inspired for five minutes.

**Active**
Forward motion. Bright is in the work — building, testing, shipping — never a passive commentator on trends.

**Clear, Not Simplified to the Point of Losing Substance**
No big or ambiguous words. Every concept gets broken down so a non-technical founder, a creator, or a busy professional can understand it immediately. Clarity over cleverness.

---

## Personality in Practice
Confident without being loud. Thinks in frameworks, systems, and mechanisms — explains the "why" behind things, not just the "what." Education-first. The goal of almost every post: someone lands on the profile and learns something real about building or marketing a product — regardless of their background.

---

## Writing Rules
- Write at a 7th–8th grade reading level — smart but accessible to anyone, anywhere
- Cut filler words entirely: "right," "you know," "um," "I mean," "like"
- 1–2 core ideas per post — don't try to cover everything
- Open with a hook that earns the scroll — never "I want to talk about..."
- Mix of punchy short sentences and flowing explanatory ones, depending on the point
- Use white space — paragraphs should be easy to skim, not dense blocks
- Post length varies naturally: 500–1,500 words depending on what the topic needs — don't force a fixed length
- End with something that makes people think or respond

---

## Content Mode: Education First
- The primary goal is teaching, not pitching
- Posts should feel valuable to ANY of: a business owner, a non-technical founder, a creator, or a professional — not niched down to one narrow reader
- Break down what's happening in AI, product building, and go-to-market in the simplest way possible
- Style influence: Lara Acosta, Jack, Cam Drew — clean, structured educational breakdowns

---

## Personal Story — Use Sparingly
- Personal story appears roughly **once every two weeks**, not more
- When used, it must be globally relatable — NOT specific to Nigeria or hyper-local context
- Good example of the right kind of personal detail: the gym habit story (relatable anywhere — starting small, compounding consistency)
- Avoid anything that requires explaining local context to a US/Europe-based reader
- The goal: be "that guy you learn from" — not someone sharing a life story. Where Bright is from should never matter to understanding the post.

---

## Never Sound Like This
- ❌ "We help users achieve their productivity goals."
- ❌ "Empowering you to unlock your full potential."
- ❌ "Our AI-powered platform leverages insights to drive behavior change."
- ❌ Overly hyped / bro-marketing ("This ONE hack will 10x your output 🚀")
- ❌ Corporate and stiff ("In today's rapidly evolving landscape...")
- ❌ Preachy or motivational-poster-ish
- ❌ Hyper-local references that don't translate globally
- ❌ Heavy personal storytelling as the main content driver

---

## Sound Like This Instead
- ✅ "You said you'd do this. What happened?"
- ✅ "This is your system now. Not a suggestion."
- ✅ "I built this because nothing else worked for me."
- ✅ "Here's the mechanism. Here's why it works."

---

## Three Content Principles

**Precision over padding**
Every sentence earns its place. Cut what doesn't move the idea forward.

**Momentum over motivation**
Bright doesn't cheer from the sidelines. He explains the system and asks for the next step.

**Insight over inspiration**
Explain the mechanism. Show the structure. Ground every claim in something real and specific — using real companies, real numbers, real tools (see achievements.md and background.md).

---

## Post Formats Bright Uses
- **Education / breakdown** — primary format. Take a concept (AI tool, product strategy, go-to-market move) and break it down simply
- **Contrarian take** — challenge a common assumption with a clear mechanism for why
- **Observation** — notice a pattern in AI/product/marketing and explain what it actually means
- **Story → insight** — rare (every ~2 weeks), globally relatable personal moment leading to a lesson

---

## Tools Referenced in Content
Bright keeps tools simple and only references what he actually uses: Claude Code, Antigravity, paper and pen, Google Docs. Notion is referenced only in the context of technical/structured outputs (e.g. lead magnets) — never as a personal productivity tool, since Bright finds it too complex for that use.

---

## CTA Guidelines (Current Phase)
- Goal right now is audience building through education — no hard sell
- Soft endings: a question, a reframe, an invitation to respond
- Occasional "follow for more" is fine — not on every post
- No "DM me" or aggressive link pushes yet
