# Mac's Voice & Rules

## The Core Idea
Mac's voice sits at the intersection of builder, strategist, and operator. It's the voice of someone who designs systems in the morning and thinks about distribution by afternoon — sharp, direct, and commercially aware.

Not theoretical. Not abstract. Always grounded in action and outcome.

---

## Voice Pillars

**Direct**
Say the thing. No padding, no softening, no filler. Short, high-impact sentences that move ideas forward. When explaining something, break it into practical mechanisms — how it works, why it matters, how it applies.

**Warm**
Not corporate. Not a motivational poster. Human. Like someone who genuinely wants to see you follow through — a mentor who builds things, not a speaker who comforts them.

**Active**
The voice should feel like forward motion. Avoid language that positions Mac as a passive observer or commentator. He's in it — building, testing, shipping.

**Personal**
Built from real experience. Reference that origin. Avoid advice that sounds borrowed from a listicle or a productivity framework never personally tested.

---

## Personality in Practice
Confident without being loud. Avoids hype language and leans on clarity, insight, and grounded ambition. Thinks in frameworks, systems, and mechanisms — and the language reflects that structured mindset.

Creativity and execution blend. The tone stays builder-first but allows expressive phrasing when describing vision, process, or a real moment. Challenge thinking rather than comfort it.

---

## Writing Rules
- Write at a 7th–8th grade reading level — smart but accessible
- Max 1–2 ideas per post — don't try to say everything
- Open with a hook that earns the scroll — no "I want to talk about..."
- Short sentences. White space. One idea per line where it helps.
- Narrative over bullet dumps — story first, list second
- Every sentence should earn its place — if a word doesn't move the idea forward, cut it
- End with something that makes people think or respond — a question, a reframe, a provocative close

---

## Never Sound Like This
- ❌ "We help users achieve their productivity goals."
- ❌ "Empowering you to unlock your full potential."
- ❌ "Our AI-powered platform leverages insights to drive behavior change."
- ❌ "It's all about creating synergies between your goals and habits."
- ❌ Overly hyped / bro-marketing ("This ONE hack will 10x your output 🚀")
- ❌ Corporate and stiff ("In today's rapidly evolving landscape...")
- ❌ Preachy or motivational-poster-ish ("Believe in yourself and others will too")
- ❌ Generic productivity advice that could apply to any tool or any person

---

## Sound Like This Instead
- ✅ "You said you'd do this. What happened?"
- ✅ "This is your system now. Not a suggestion."
- ✅ "I built this because nothing else worked for me."
- ✅ "Here's the mechanism. Here's why it works."

---

## Three Content Principles

**Precision over padding**
Every sentence earns its place. Cut what doesn't move the idea forward.

**Momentum over motivation**
Mac doesn't cheer from the sidelines. He stands in the way of excuses and asks for the next step.

**Insight over inspiration**
Explain the mechanism. Show the system. Ground every claim in something real and specific.

---

## Post Formats Mac Uses
- **Story → insight** — open with a real moment, land on a lesson
- **Contrarian take** — challenge a common assumption with evidence and a mechanism
- **Results-led** — lead with a real outcome, then unpack how
- **Observation** — notice something happening in the space and explain what it means

---

## Case Studies & Proof Points
Mac builds products and uses them as real examples in content. These appear as proof of what's possible — never as pitches. The product serves the story, not the other way around.

---

## CTA Guidelines (Current Phase)
- Goal right now is audience building — no hard sell
- Soft endings: a question, a reframe, an invitation to respond
- Occasional "follow for more" is fine — not on every post
- No "DM me" or aggressive link pushes yet
