# LinkedIn Post Skill

Turn a raw idea, observation, or topic into a polished LinkedIn post in Mac's voice.

## How to Use
```
/linkedin-post [your idea, thought, or topic]
```

Examples:
- `/linkedin-post most founders don't fail because of the idea, they fail because they can't stay consistent`
- `/linkedin-post I just tested three AI coding tools back to back — here's what actually happened`
- `/linkedin-post the non-technical founder has more leverage right now than ever`

---

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Understand the Idea
Parse what Mac is trying to say:
- What's the core insight or observation?
- Is there a story behind it — something that happened, something he tested, something he noticed?
- Who is this most relevant to in his audience?

Ask Mac for more context if the idea is too thin to write something real.

### Step 2 — Do Light Research
Before drafting, do a quick search:
- Is there a real trend, data point, or conversation that supports or adds texture to this idea?
- Is anyone else talking about this — and if so, what angle is Mac uniquely positioned to take?

Use research to add one specific, grounded detail that makes the post feel real — not just an opinion.

### Step 3 — Read the References
Open `references/reference.md` and note:
- The post formats Mac likes
- The tone and rhythm he's going for
- Any patterns that have worked well

Match that standard.

### Step 4 — Draft the Post

Follow Mac's voice rules (from `../lead-magnet/references/voice-and-rules.md`):
- Direct, conversational, story-driven
- Easy to read — short sentences, white space
- Opens with a hook that earns the scroll
- One or two ideas max — don't try to say everything
- Ends with something that makes people think or respond

### Post Structure Options

**SLAY Framework (Story-Based)**
```
[Opening hook that drops the reader directly into a story (Story - S)]

[Unpack the core challenge, test, or experience (Story - S)]

[Explain the key realizations or lessons learned from that story (Lessons - L)]

[Provide 2-3 specific actionable steps the reader can take (Actionable - A, formatted as 1/  , 2/  )]

[Ask a direct personal question or advise what they should do next (You - Y)]
```

**Contrarian Take**
```
[Bold opening statement that challenges something people assume]

[Why the common belief exists]

[What Mac has seen / experienced / built that contradicts it]

[The real principle underneath]

[Closing — invite them to push back or share their take]
```

**Results Led**
```
[Open with a real outcome or result]

[What led to it — the decision, system, or approach]

[The specific thing that made the difference]

[What this means for someone reading this]

[Soft close]
```

### Step 5 — Present the Post

Output:
1. The drafted post, formatted with proper line breaks for LinkedIn
2. A one-line note on the format used and why it fits the idea
3. Optional: a short alternate opening line if the hook could go a different direction
